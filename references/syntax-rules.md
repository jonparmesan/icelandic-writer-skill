# Syntax Rules Reference (Setningafræði)

## V2 Rule — Main Clauses

The finite verb MUST be the second constituent in declarative sentences.

### Standard Patterns

| Pattern | Example | Gloss |
|---------|---------|-------|
| SVO (default) | Ég borða fisk | I eat fish |
| AdvVS | Í dag borða ég ekki fisk | Today eat I not fish |
| OVS (topicalized) | Fiskinn borða ég ekki | THE FISH eat I not |

### Important Details

- Conjunctions (og, en, eða, af því að) occupy a "null" position — they don't count for V2
- Questions: verb moves to first position (V1): Borðar þú fisk?
- Imperatives: also V1: Borðaðu fiskinn!

## V2 in Embedded Clauses (Icelandic A vs B)

Icelandic is an "I-V2" language — V2 can apply even in embedded clauses (unlike Danish/Swedish).

### Type A (V2 in embedded): Verb precedes adverbs

- Ég veit að Jón les **oft** bókina
- (I know that John reads often the book)

### Type B (V-late in embedded): Adverbs precede verb

- Ég veit að Jón **oft** les bókina
- (I know that John often reads the book)

Both are grammatical. Type A is more common in speech; Type B is more formal.

## Topicalization

Moving a constituent to the front for emphasis, triggering subject-verb inversion:

- **Bókina** las ég í gær (THE BOOK read I yesterday)
- **Á morgun** fer ég til Akureyrar (TOMORROW go I to Akureyri)

### Restrictions

- Restricted in embedded clauses
- Only works with "bridge verbs" (segja, telja, halda) in embedded context:
  - Hann segir að **bókina** hafi Jón lesið (He says that THE BOOK has John read)

## Stylistic Fronting

Unique to Icelandic. Moves elements before the finite verb when there's a subject gap (the subject position is empty, typically in relative clauses or after impersonal constructions).

### Examples

- Maðurinn sem **farinn** var heim... (The man who gone was home...)
- ...sem **ekki** er hægt að hafna (...which not is possible to reject)
- Þetta er bók sem **vel** er skrifuð (This is a book that well is written)

### Accessibility Hierarchy

What can be fronted, in order of preference:

1. **Negation** (ekki, aldrei) — most easily fronted
2. **Adverbs** (vel, oft, alltaf)
3. **Past participle / Verb particle** (farinn, kominn, upp, niður)
4. **Predicative adjective** (tilbúinn, hægt)

### Conditions

- Requires a subject gap (empty subject position)
- Does NOT change meaning — manages rhythm and flow
- More common in formal/literary prose
- Only one element can be fronted at a time

## Negation and Adverb Positioning

### Main Clauses

Standard position: after both finite verb and subject

- Ég borða **ekki** fisk (I eat not fish)
- Jón hefur **aldrei** séð það (John has never seen that)

### Embedded Clauses

- Type A (V2): Verb before adverb — ...að Jón les **ekki** bókina
- Type B (V-late): Adverb before verb — ...að Jón **ekki** les bókina

### With Stylistic Fronting

Negation/adverbs can appear before the verb:
- ...sem **ekki** er hægt að gera (...which not is possible to do)
